Vandenberg Internship

During the summer of 2023, I had the wonderful opportunity to be a STEM intern at Vandenberg Space Force Base with the Arctic Slopes Regional Corporation.

For 10 weeks, I worked under two direct report squadrons at Space Launch Delta 30 (SLD 30)/Vandenberg SFB

2nd Range Operations Squadron (2ROPS) and the 2nd Space Launch Squadron (2SLS)

For the first five weeks of my internship, I worked with 2ROPS, a range squadron dedicated to ensuring range safety. This team works with all subsystems that contribute to a safe launch pad including weather, telemetry, optics, communication, leadership, etc. My job had me involved in server rooms and remote control centers that mainly control the interfaces that launch rockets out of base. These missions include the Minuteman Intercontinental Ballistic Missiles, SpaceX, Firefly Aerospace, and Naval Air Warfare Center missions. I was also responsible in helping make a schedule that includes all launches and testing that would be happening on base.

For the latter weeks of my internship, I moved over to 2SLS. Their mission is to deliver exceptional mission assurance, integration, maintenance, and operations for space launch, landing and test missions. I had the opportunity to design and present a feasibility analysis of Space Launch Complex 6 and SpaceX's Starship spacecraft and booster. A lot of research went into environmental analyses, fuel, transportation, and acoustics. All the research made was open-source meaning that no numbers or data was taken from SpaceX's records and everything was found online through the internet. Along with my fellow co-intern, not only have we made a full presentation, but we also wrote an entire paper that goes into detail of all the research done.
