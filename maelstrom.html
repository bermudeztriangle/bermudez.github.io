Project Maelstrom

Project Maelstrom is Cal Poly Pomona's e-VTOL Program. Development of the vehicle has matured to its preliminary design phase and has made its first maiden flight during the spring semester of 2022.

My job as a student iron-bird engineer is purely hands-on as I would work with power tools and aircraft materials. Our project is still in the preliminary phase and we have only created the first version. Version 1 has already had its maiden flight despite it only being made of foam board and wood. It was able to hover about one foot in the air before coming down due to flimsy material. As of Fall 2023, we have started building version 2 of the aircraft in hopes of another successful flight.

Working with about 40 other students and 12 in my subteam from a variety of disciplines has been an incredible experience.

Currently, the team and I are working on building the next model for our project. The picture on the right is of a hot wire foam cutter that I built being heated up by a power supply. With this, we can cut foam into NACA0012 airfoils to be used as templates for our carbon fiber airfoil.
