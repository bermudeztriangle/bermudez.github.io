Balloon Launch Assessment Directive for Everyone

The Balloon Launch Assessment Directive for Everyone, or BLADE, is the quickest way for students to gain the most experience.

As the team leader of a team consisting of 7 first-year multidisciplinary engineering students, my responsibility was to design a 1-U CubeSat. The project's primary objective was to assess and quantify ground-level light pollution. We underwent an extensive process, including several presentations such as CoDR, PDR, CDR, and other showcases, which were all delivered to industry professionals. This particular project holds a special place in my heart as it presented valuable lessons, despite falling slightly short of expectations. The experience served as a catalyst for my personal growth, motivating me to strive for excellence. Consequently, for the academic year of 2023-2024, I assumed the role of Engineering Coordinator within Bronco Space's esteemed Executive Board.

Working with about 40 other students and 12 in my subteam from a variety of disciplines has been an incredible experience.

Currently, the team and I are working on building the next model for our project. The picture on the right is of a hot wire foam cutter that I built being heated up by a power supply. With this, we can cut foam into NACA0012 airfoils to be used as templates for our carbon fiber airfoil.
